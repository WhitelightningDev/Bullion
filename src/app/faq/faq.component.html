Gold, Silver, or Platinum pools (i.e. the unit prices) are valued at the daily wholesale price (which is priced around a 5% premium over the London PM Fix). A member buys in and sells at this price. The admin fee and commission are added when buying in.

  • Buying elsewhere may cost 10% or more over the London PM Fix. Selling may yield less.
  • The unitized system ensures members rarely sell for less than wholesale (London PM Fix +5%).
  • Trade cost is reduced to ~0.15%, significantly lower than competitors.
  • Bullion offers full transparency and low pricing for members.
  • Traditional unit trusts cannot hold physical precious metals due to FAIS restrictions. Bullion fills that gap.

Redemption: Members can redeem units for cash, gold, silver, platinum — or any combination.

Physical Stock: Most gold, silver, and platinum is stored securely at Knox Vault.

  • Vault access is protected by eye-recognition technology and must be pre-arranged.
  • Stock insurance is underwritten by Zurich, approved by Bullion members at general meetings.
  • Visits allowed only for:
    • Adding precious metals
    • Withdrawing precious metals
    • Auditing vault contents
  • Every vault visit is accompanied by a Bullion Board member and an auditor representative.
  • Formal stock audits are performed at least once a year.

CO-OPERATIVE:

This is a very secure business vehicle for holding a member's precious metal assets. The Co-operative has the right of possession, while members share ownership of the pools according to the number of units allocated to them. Therefore, creditors of the co-operative, if any, are not entitled to lay claim to any precious metals or cash that is being held on behalf of the members.

The Co-operative has no creditors apart from the bank for bank charges and the auditors. Its only liability is to the members for their units.

Investment Requirements:

  • Minimum lump sum tranches of R5000 (initial or additional amounts). This may be amended from time to time.
  • Minimum debit order of R500 per month.
  • There is no maximum investment limit.

Unitising stock and cash in a pool offers many advantages:

  • Reduces entry/exit costs from over 5% to as low as ~0.15% per trade.
  • Accommodates round amounts without precise metal pricing (e.g. R100,000).
  • Allows smaller investments (e.g. R500 debit order) that still gain exposure to metals.
  • Enables flexible cash withdrawals (e.g. redeem R4,500 from R545,678.98 value).
  • Insurance, admin, and safekeeping costs are paid by the pool, not the member.
  • Enables seamless switching between pools at wholesale pricing (~0.25% cost).
  • Avoids typical commissions and price reductions associated with metal sales.

Note: Unitizing avoids cross-subsidizing — each deposit/withdrawal occurs at the unit price of the day.

Unitizing is a method used to as accurately as possible keep record of each investor's equitable share in a pool of assets. It ensures that a new investor's share is based on the price he joins the pool and investors in that pool share pro-rata in the costs of that pool.

Example:

Day 1

  • Pool X starts at R1.00 per unit.
  • Investor A invests R100,000, receiving 100,000 units at R1.00 each.
  • The pool uses R99,000 to purchase 11 Krugerrands at R9,000 each.
  • Remaining cash = R1,000.

Day 2

  • Krugerrands are priced at R9,200.
  • Krugerrands value: 11 x R9,200 = R101,200
  • Cash: R1,000
  • Total Pool Value = R102,200
  • Unit Price = R102,200 / 100,000 = R1.022 per unit
  • Investor A's holding = 100,000 x R1.022 = R102,200

Mr Z invests R100,000 on Day 2

  • He receives R100,000 / R1.022 = 97,847.36 units
  • Total units in pool = 197,847.36 units
  • The pool buys 10 Krugerrands at R9,200 = R92,000

Pool Composition Now:

  • Krugerrands: 21 x R9,200 = R193,200
  • Cash: R9,000
  • Total Pool Value = R202,200
  • Unit Price = R202,200 / 197,847.36 = R1.022

Each member's share is now calculated accordingly based on units held and unit price.

    When the pool is not fully invested in precious metals and holds a portion in cash, fluctuations in metal prices will cause the unit price to change at a slower rate than the actual price of the metals. This may result in a slight positive or negative shift in the metal composition. The co-op generally aims to maintain 3% to 10% of the pool in cash for operational flexibility.

Income Tax:
None. Since no interest is earned, income tax is not applicable — either on the member’s holdings or membership fees.

Capital Gains Tax (CGT):
No CGT is triggered when a member withdraws their member's interest in the form of physical gold, silver, or platinum. However, should the member sell the precious metals thereafter or redeem units for cash, CGT may apply and the member will be responsible for reporting such gains or losses to SARS.

Profits or losses realized through trading pool redemptions will also be subject to CGT in the hands of the member. Bullion itself carries no CGT liability.

Value Added Tax (VAT):
No VAT applies to Krugerrands.
VAT does apply to silver and platinum. As a VAT-registered entity, Bullion may reclaim INPUT VAT on purchases of silver and platinum. These VAT refunds may then be used to reinvest in more precious metals on behalf of the members.

If Bullion sells any silver or platinum, VAT OUTPUT tax must be paid to SARS.

If a member requests delivery of their silver or platinum, they will be charged VAT OUTPUT tax on the delivery. This amount will be paid to SARS by Bullion. However, if the member is VAT-registered, they may be able to reclaim this VAT as INPUT tax.
